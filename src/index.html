Why Study MBBS Abroad?

Key Benefits

  • Many countries like Russia, Ukraine, the Philippines, and Kazakhstan offer MBBS at lower costs compared to private medical colleges in India.
  • Medical universities abroad are often recognized by WHO, NMC (MCI), ECFMG, and FAIMER.
  • Universities have modern labs, research facilities, and experienced faculty.
  • Many universities admit students based on 12th-grade marks without the need for additional entrance exams (except NEET for Indian students to practice in India).
  • Opportunity to interact with students and doctors from different countries.
  • Clinical training in world-class hospitals provides hands-on experience.

Admission Process & Eligibility

The process of applying for an MBBS program abroad varies by country and university, but here's a general step-by-step guide:

  1. Research & Choose the Right Country & University
  2. Check Eligibility Criteria
    • Age: Minimum 17 years (as of December 31 of admission year).
    • 50% marks in PCB (Physics, Chemistry, Biology) for General category.
    • 40% marks for SC/ST/OBC (as per NMC rules for Indian students).
  3. Appear for University/Entrance Exam (if required)
  4. Application & Document Submission
  5. Receive Admission Letter (Offer Letter)
  6. Apply for Student Visa
  7. Pay Tuition Fees & Book Flight Tickets
  8. Travel & Start MBBS Journey
